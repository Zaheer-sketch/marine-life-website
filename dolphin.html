About Dolphins

Dolphins are highly intelligent marine mammals known for their playful behavior and social structures. They are part of the cetacean family, which includes whales and porpoises.

Habitat

Dolphins can be found in oceans and seas around the world, typically in shallow waters near the coast, but some species also inhabit deeper waters.

Diet

Dolphins primarily feed on fish and squid, using echolocation to hunt their prey.

Social Structure

Dolphins are known for their complex social structures, often living in groups called pods. They communicate with each other using a variety of sounds and body language.

Conservation Status

While some dolphin populations are stable, others are threatened by habitat loss, pollution, and fishing practices. Conservation efforts are essential to protect these intelligent creatures.
