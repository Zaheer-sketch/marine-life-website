Plastic pollution is a major threat to marine life. Millions of tons of plastic enter the oceans each year, harming wildlife and ecosystems. Marine animals often ingest plastic or become entangled in it.

Effects of Plastic Pollution

Plastic can break down into microplastics, which are consumed by small marine organisms and can enter the food chain, posing health risks to humans and marine life alike.

Combating Plastic Pollution

Efforts to combat plastic pollution include reducing plastic use, increasing recycling efforts, and supporting legislation aimed at reducing plastic waste. Community clean-up events and awareness campaigns are also effective.
