Coral reefs are vital ecosystems that support a diverse range of marine life. They provide habitat, food, and protection for many species. Unfortunately, they are threatened by climate change, pollution, and overfishing.

Importance of Coral Reefs

Coral reefs are often referred to as the "rainforests of the sea" due to their biodiversity. They play a crucial role in coastal protection and support fisheries that millions of people rely on for their livelihoods.

How to Protect Coral Reefs

Protecting coral reefs involves reducing carbon emissions, minimizing pollution, and supporting sustainable fishing practices. Community involvement and awareness are also essential for conservation efforts.
